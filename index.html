Introduction

Over my many years of travelling around the world, I have encountered many things, both marvelous and dangerous. As a well-versed traveller, I collected whatever souvenier I could to remember these encounters. This site contains some of the many items I've brought back with me, both big and small...actually, that's a lie, all these items are small. As small as they may be, they all carry their own story. Take a gander at the marvelous collection before you and see for yourself!

Egyptian Camel Phone

This story is a doozy. There I was in the harsh Egyptian desert, alone on a camelback. I was almost out of water and there wasn't a soul in sight. Desperate for help, I tried to use my phone to call emergency services, but it slipped out of my sweaty hands and the damn camel stepped on it! I made it out alive of course, being the dashing and handsome man that I am, but it was definitely an experience that gave me quite the fright! Lesson learnt, never use your phone and ride a camelback.

Chinese Vial of Venom

This vial of venom was acquired from 'milking' a King Cobra. Snakes are not a rare sight there, especially in the rural areas. Despite how commonplace they are to see, they're still dangerous, however. I actually got bitten by one and almost didn't make it when the hospital didn't have the right antidote! which is exactly why I had to milk it of its venom. That way, I can make the antidote myself if I ever get bitten by a snake again, it's a genius and fool-proof plan!

Australian Outback Gem

Australia is known for being a mining country, so is it really a surprise that I was able to get my hands on a real beauty of a diamond? I fought tooth and nail for this and then had to make a daring escape through a collapsing tunnel. I almost didn't make it! I lived, fortunately, and I was able to make back with this stunning gem. I had it cut, of course, and now it stands as one of my most prized valuables in this collection. Don't get any ideas though....

Pakistani Mosquito Repellant

Now, you may be wondering, why on Earth is this in a collection of memorable travel items? Well, you see, mosquitos are quite the problem abroad, literally coming in droves. I thought I was being smart when I got this mosquito repellant from a local store owner. All you have to do to use it is apply a single drop or dot of it onto your shoulders and thighs. I did as per the instructions and sure enough, I had a bout of malaria within the week.

French Butterfly Knife

This item comes from one of my more violent encounters. This knife made its way into my thigh after I found myself in an attempted mugging. I say attempted because I fought those hooligans with everything I had! With the scoundrels knockd out cold, I had to rush to the ER for the copious amounts of blood I was losing due to this rusty knife. It's nothing special, really, just like any other buttefly knife, but the memory it carries is just as thrilling as it was painful.

British Bulletshells

Another case where I had to get my hands dirty. These bullets are from actually from genuine 1945 longarm rifles, which makes it all the more confusing as to how a group of British thugs got their hands on it. I took care of them, of course, but I was unforunately unable to salvage the rifles. The oafs broke it while trying to hit me over the head with it! All I could gather from that encounter were these bulletshells, a reminder that no matter which era it's from, a bullet will put you out all the same

Indian Ganesha Idol

Finally, an item in this collection that isn't related to me dancing with death! This is an idol of the Hindu Deity Ganesha, made of a soft sandstone. Ganesha is often known as the God of Success and Wisdom, all things that I already am. This idol was gifted to me by the locals as a token of our friendship and I still hold it dear to this day. The idol brings back pleasant memories of my travels in India and makes me long to go back one day.

Assorted Coins

To top off this collection, coins! Who doesn't like coins? These aren't from any particular country, rather they're from different places and eras. Some of these places include Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, Hong Kong and even Imperial India. In fact, some of these coins date as far back as 1835! For those of you who don't know, that's during the time the British occupied the country and it shows, given how the coins feel as though they're from an entirely different country, but I digress.
